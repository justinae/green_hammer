SW Portland LEED Platinum Gut-Rehab

One of the nations first LEED Platinum remodels recently completed by Green Hammer in collaboration with the Paolo Design Group won the 2009 Oregon Remodelers Association Outstanding Remodeling Achievement Award for best Green Building Remodel. This outstanding project is an excellent testament to designer Paul Scardina "Paolo" ability to transform an outdated dysfunctional and inefficient space into one of our nations premier examples of high performance home conversion and how to maximize spaces while giving them a grand and elegant feel. SE Stephen's Street Remodel

This was an incredibly fun design build process with our clients at Bloom Design. All of the wood used for the new floors, countertops, kitchen shelving, interior trim, barrel vaulted, straight vault, and wave shaped ceilings, we had custom milled from salvaged pine bleacher seats sourced from a nearby deconstruction project. Building the various styles of vaults were the most fun and of course my favorite was the wave shaped ceiling in the master bedroom! See the portfolio.

Flavel Street Remodel

This is a hand framed post and beam second story addition to a 1920's bungalow in Sellwood. All of the wood sourced for the project, from building lumber to trim, is from the Healthy Forests Healthy Communities partnership, salvaged, and/or from local FSC certified forests. Includes a hand crafted Ofuro wood tub in the master bath! See the portfolio.

SE Taggart Remodel

This was a very run down single family home that was built in the 1920's and at some point in it's life been illegally converted to a duplex and nearly destroyed. With the help of owners and designers from Bloom Design we restored this home back to a single family home using primarily material salvaged from the site and elements salvaged from the deconstruction at our SE Stephen's Project.
